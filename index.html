Various Animal Facts

Cat Facts

  • Cats are the most popular pet in the United States: There are 88 million pet cats and 74 million dogs.
  • There are cats who have survived falls from over 32 stories (320 meters) onto concrete.
  • A group of cats is called a clowder.
  • Cats have five toes on their front paws, but only four on their back paws.
  • Cats have a third eyelid called a “haw”.

Dog Facts

  • Dogs are as smart as a two-year-old child.
  • There are over 340 different dog breeds worldwide.
  • A dog's sense of smell is 10,000 times stronger than humans.
  • Greyhounds are the fastest dogs on Earth and can run at speeds of 45 mph.
  • Three dogs survived the sinking of the Titanic: two Pomeranians and one Pekingese.

Bear Facts

  • Bears can run up to 40 miles per hour, fast enough to catch a running horse.
  • Bears are very good swimmers and have been known to swim long distances across lakes or seas.
  • Despite their heavy build and awkward gait, bears are adept runners, climbers, and swimmers.
  • Bears can stand on their hind legs for 10-15 seconds.
  • Bears have an excellent sense of smell, better than dogs or possibly any other mammal.
